Membership Requirements

The following are requirements for anyone wishing to apply to the BCA Chapter of the NHS:

  • Scholarship: Minimum 3.500 GPA at the end of sophomore/junior year as reflected on Naviance (3.499 is not 3.500).
  • Leadership: Demonstrated leadership in and out of school with leadership qualities, skills, and experience (e.g. Vice President of Class Council).
  • Service: At least 40 completed hours of community service for applying juniors and seniors (only 10 hours can be completed in-house).
  • Character: Demonstrating trustworthiness, respect, responsibility, fairness, caring, and citizenship (student complies with school regulations, avoids cheating, and takes criticism willingly).

NHS members must meet the following requirements at the end of junior year:

  • A cumulative GPA of at least 3.500.
  • At least 40 hours of community service.
  • Completion of at least 7 tutoring hours (these do not count towards community service) that were performed during junior year.
  • Completion 4 hours during second trimester and 3 hours during third trimester.
  • Your hours must be logged by the end of each trimester.
  • Participation in at least one internal BCA service project and one external NHS service project during junior year.

NHS members must meet the following requirements at the end of senior year:

  • A cumulative GPA of at least 3.500.
  • At least 50 hours of community service.
  • Completion of at least 10 tutoring hours (these do not count towards community service) that were performed during senior year.
  • You must complete 3 hours during first trimester, 4 hours during second trimester, and 3 hours during third trimester. This means that newly inducted seniors only have to complete 6 hours, since they were not in the NHS during first trimester.
  • Participation in at least one internal BCA service project and one external NHS service project during senior year.

If your application is denied, here are possible reasons:

  • Your application may be denied because your cumulative GPA was not at least a 3.500, hence missing the pillar of scholarship.
  • Your application may be denied because the number of completed community service hours was not at least 40 hours.
  • Your application may be denied because your essay on scholarship did not demonstrate the qualities of scholarship. Scholarship is not only measured in oneâ€™s GPA; it is the enjoyment of learning and the passion for knowledge.
  • Your application may be denied because your essay on leadership did not demonstrate the qualities of a leader. Leadership is exhibited in managing multiple components and diverse people toward a common goal. Leaders typically gain their skills and experience through leadership positions in and out of school (e.g. Vice President of Class Council).
  • Your application may be denied because your essay on service did not demonstrate service. Service is exhibited in cheerfully assisting visitors, teachers, and students and volunteering dependable and well-organized assistance.
  • Your application may be denied because your essay on character did not demonstrate character. A student of character is one who demonstrates trustworthiness, respect, responsibility, fairness, caring, and citizenship. The student complies with school regulations, avoids cheating and plagiarism, and takes criticism willingly.

Appeal Guidelines:

  • Juniors: Due to the large volume of applications received each year, appeals made by Juniors will not be heard by the Faculty Council. Please reapply next fall.
  • Seniors: If you wish to submit an appeal, please write a formal letter of appeal and submit it to Mrs. Lawler at 200 Hackensack Ave, Hackensack, NJ 07601 within 10 days of receiving the letter of non-admission.

NHS Induction Make-Up:

The Bergen County Academies Chapter of NHS requires attendance at the Induction Ceremony for all newly selected candidates per Article VIII, Section 4 of the national constitution. Should circumstances such as illness or personal emergency or other legitimate extenuating circumstances prevent a candidate from attending the scheduled ceremony, an informal induction should be held for the candidate at a later date.
